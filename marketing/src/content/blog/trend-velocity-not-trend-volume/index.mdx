# Trend velocity, not trend volume

## Working angle

- Core promise: explain that Digest Engine helps readers spot momentum early, instead of pointing them toward topics that are already exhausted.
- Audience lens: marketers and newsletter authors who need to publish while a story is becoming important, not after everyone else has already covered it.
- Tone: practical and editorial. The article should feel like better issue-planning guidance, not a lesson in analytics.
- Suggested headline direction: *Catch trends on the way up, not on the way out.*

## 1. Open with the problem of late trend discovery

- Start with the familiar frustration: by the time something shows up in most "trending" tools, it is often already everywhere.
- Frame the editorial cost: if a topic is already saturated, covering it may not add much value for readers unless you have a very different angle.
- Set up the main distinction: volume tells you what is already big, but velocity tells you what is rapidly becoming important.

## 2. Define trend velocity in plain language

- Explain that Digest Engine is not just looking for topics that are mentioned a lot.
- It is looking for topics whose rate of attention is increasing.
- Use a simple contrast example: a topic mentioned six times every day is less urgent than one that jumped from two mentions yesterday to twelve today.
- Keep this section intuitive and non-mathematical.

## 3. Explain why volume alone is not enough

- Popularity can be a lagging signal.
- Raw mention count tends to surface stories that are already obvious, widely discussed, or nearing exhaustion.
- Emphasize that for newsletters and marketing content, the real advantage often comes from finding the topic just before it becomes unavoidable.
- This section should help readers see why "most mentioned" is not the same as "most useful to cover next."

## 4. Show what Digest Engine does differently

- Explain that the system looks across the project’s incoming content and highlights clusters of topics gaining momentum.
- Mention that this works across the sources already feeding the project, not just a single social platform.
- Explain that the system turns these rising clusters into more useful editorial signals, not just a score on a chart.

## 5. Introduce theme suggestions as the editor-facing output

- Explain that accelerating topics are grouped into suggested newsletter themes.
- These themes help the editor quickly understand why a trend matters and what related stories belong together.
- Position themes as the bridge between trend detection and actual newsletter planning.
- Keep this section grounded in the editorial workflow: the product is turning signals into usable issue structure.

## 6. Give a realistic scenario

- Walk through an example relevant to marketers or newsletter authors.
- Example shape: one topic has been steadily discussed for weeks, while another suddenly begins appearing across multiple sources over the last few days.
- Show how a volume-based tool would keep surfacing the older saturated topic, while Digest Engine would flag the accelerating one.
- End the scenario by showing how that helps the editor publish with better timing.

## 7. Explain why this matters for marketers and newsletter authors specifically

- Marketers need to know when a conversation is beginning to take shape, not only when it has already peaked.
- Newsletter authors need a better way to identify what deserves attention this week, not what generated the most noise over the last month.
- Emphasize that velocity helps the reader spend their limited issue space on signals with real momentum.

## 8. Address the likely objection

- Question to address: "Does chasing velocity just create reactive content?"
- Answer: not if the signal is used well.
- Explain that trend velocity is not a command to publish everything fast. It is an early-warning system that helps the editor decide whether to cover, ignore, or frame a trend differently.
- Keep this section short and pragmatic.

## 9. Make the longer-view point

- Explain that this is not only about 24-hour spikes.
- Digest Engine can retain enough history to distinguish between fleeting noise and a topic that is genuinely building.
- Position this as a way to track momentum over time, not just chase novelty for its own sake.

## 10. Close with the takeaway

- Re-state the practical benefit: trend velocity helps readers find the stories that are gaining energy before they become stale.
- Tie it back to editorial outcomes: better timing, stronger theme planning, and more distinctive issues.
- End with a bridge to adjacent features like competitive intelligence or draft assembly.

## Notes to preserve from source material

- The core distinction between acceleration and raw mention count should stay central.
- Theme suggestions are an important downstream benefit and should remain part of the final article.
- The eventual article should preserve the idea that Digest Engine helps identify multi-day momentum, not just one-day spikes.
- Avoid detailed formulas or ratios in the final piece unless they are translated into intuitive editorial language.
