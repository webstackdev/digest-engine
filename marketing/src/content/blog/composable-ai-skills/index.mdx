# Composable AI skills

## Working angle

- Core promise: explain that Digest Engine does not treat AI as one black box. Instead, it uses focused capabilities for specific editorial jobs.
- Audience lens: marketers and newsletter authors who want practical help, not mysterious automation.
- Tone: clear, confident, and reassuring. Emphasize control, flexibility, and usefulness over technical novelty.
- Suggested headline direction: *Every AI capability is a tool you can call by name.*

## 1. Open with the common frustration around AI products

- Start with the problem readers already recognize: many AI tools feel like a single vague assistant that does a little of everything and explains none of it.
- Frame the trust issue: if the system cannot tell you what it is doing, it is hard to rely on it in a publishing workflow.
- Set up Digest Engine’s difference: instead of one monolithic AI feature, it breaks the work into specific capabilities that each do one job well.

## 2. Define "composable AI skills" in plain language

- Explain that a skill is just a focused AI task inside the product.
- Give concrete examples: classifying a piece, checking relevance, removing duplicates, generating a summary, extracting entities, parsing newsletters, or grouping themes.
- Make the idea approachable: each skill helps with one step of the editorial process, rather than pretending to replace the whole process.
- Emphasize that this makes the system easier to understand and easier to trust.

## 3. Explain why this matters to non-technical users

- Readers do not need to care how the AI is wired internally. They care that each capability behaves predictably.
- Show the benefit in editorial terms: when a summary looks off, you know it is the summary step, not the entire system falling apart.
- Explain that modular skills make the workflow more transparent because each part has a visible purpose.
- Position this as a usability advantage, not a technical architecture claim.

## 4. Walk through the most useful skills in the workflow

- Classification: helps label what kind of item just came in.
- Relevance scoring: helps the shortlist reflect the project’s editorial taste.
- Deduplication: prevents the same story from showing up over and over from multiple sources.
- Summarization: turns long articles into quick, readable briefings.
- Entity extraction: spots the people, companies, and products worth tracking.
- Newsletter parsing and theme detection: turn incoming material into something usable for planning and drafting.
- Keep each explanation short and tied to a user benefit.

## 5. Show what this looks like in practice

- Give a simple, realistic scenario: a marketer or newsletter author opens the queue and sees a new article.
- Show the sequence in plain terms: the system identifies what it is, judges whether it fits the project, removes duplicates, summarizes it, and surfaces the important names or themes.
- Mention that some of these capabilities can also be triggered directly in the interface when the editor wants a closer look.
- Use this section to make the feature feel tangible rather than abstract.

## 6. Explain the advantage of modularity without sounding technical

- One benefit is flexibility: the product can improve one skill without forcing the whole workflow to change.
- Another is resilience: one weak result does not have to ruin the entire process.
- Another is clarity: editors can understand why something happened instead of being asked to trust a mystery system.
- Keep this framed as reliability and control rather than engineering detail.

## 7. Address the model-choice point in user-friendly language

- Reframe "model-agnostic" for non-technical readers: Digest Engine is built so the right AI can be used for the right task.
- Explain the user-facing value: you are not locked into one vendor or one all-purpose assistant.
- Keep this practical: the product can stay adaptable as models improve, without forcing readers to relearn the workflow.
- Avoid naming too many models in the main outline unless needed as a brief supporting note.

## 8. Connect the feature to editorial trust

- Emphasize that composable skills help readers understand what the product is doing and why.
- Tie this to confidence in publishing: when you know how the system is helping, you can make better use of it.
- Reinforce that Digest Engine is designed to remove busywork while keeping judgment in human hands.

## 9. Close with the takeaway

- Re-state the practical benefit: AI works better in publishing when it is broken into understandable, useful tasks.
- Position Digest Engine as a workflow partner made of clear capabilities, not one opaque promise.
- End with a bridge to related features like human review, draft assembly, or relevance training.
