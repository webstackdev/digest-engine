# Human review by default

## Working angle

- Core promise: show that Digest Engine is built to escalate uncertainty to a human instead of pretending the AI is always right.
- Audience lens: marketers and newsletter authors who want help from AI, but do not want embarrassing mistakes or invisible failures.
- Tone: reassuring, practical, and trust-building. This article should make the reader feel safer using the product.
- Suggested headline direction: *Nothing ships without you reading it.*

## 1. Open with the trust problem

- Start with the fear many readers already have about AI in publishing: the worst mistakes often happen when a system acts confident while being wrong.
- Frame the specific editorial risk: misclassified content, bad entity matches, broken newsletter parsing, or weak recommendations can quietly damage the quality of an issue.
- Set up the core principle: Digest Engine is designed to surface uncertainty instead of hiding it.

## 2. Define "human review by default" in plain language

- Explain that when Digest Engine is not confident, it does not silently push the item through and it does not quietly discard it.
- Instead, it sends the item to a review queue where the editor can confirm, reject, or correct it.
- Keep the explanation simple: the system knows when to ask for help.
- Emphasize that this is a product design choice, not an afterthought.

## 3. Explain what kinds of things go to review

- Low-confidence classifications.
- Borderline relevance decisions.
- Ambiguous entity matches.
- Unclear or unparseable newsletter inputs.
- Possibly duplicated content that needs a human call.
- Make each example sound familiar and editorial, not technical.

## 4. Show why this matters for newsletter and marketing workflows

- Marketers and newsletter authors are not just collecting data; they are publishing judgment.
- A silent mistake can lead to irrelevant stories, wrong attributions, or low-quality sections making it into the issue.
- Human review protects the editorial bar by making the uncertain cases visible before they become output.
- Tie this section to trust in the final issue, not just trust in the software.

## 5. Walk through a realistic scenario

- Example shape: a forwarded newsletter is parsed incorrectly, or an article mentions a company name that could refer to two different things.
- Show what many AI tools would do: guess, proceed, and never tell you there was ambiguity.
- Show what Digest Engine does instead: flag the item, route it to review, and let the editor make the call.
- End the example by highlighting that this saves the editor from cleaning up hidden errors later.

## 6. Explain the feedback loop in user-friendly terms

- When the editor confirms, rejects, or corrects something, that action becomes useful signal for future runs.
- Present this simply: every review makes the system more aligned with the project over time.
- Keep the focus on practical improvement rather than training mechanics.
- Position the review queue as part of how the product learns responsibly.

## 7. Contrast this with typical AI automation

- Many tools optimize for appearing fast and seamless, even if that means burying uncertain cases.
- Digest Engine optimizes for reliability in an editorial workflow, which means making ambiguity visible.
- Explain that this is slower in the right places and faster overall because it prevents downstream cleanup.

## 8. Expand on the operational confidence angle

- Mention that review is not just about content quality. It is also about being able to see what happened when something goes wrong.
- You can describe this in user-facing language: the system is auditable, visible, and correctable.
- Keep technical logging/checkpoint details out of the main body unless they are translated into simple benefits like "you can see what happened" or "you can retry the stuck step without starting over."

## 9. Address the likely objection

- Question to address: "Doesn't review just create more work?"
- Answer: only for the cases that actually need judgment.
- Explain that the goal is not to force editors to check everything, but to pull forward the uncertain edge cases so they do not cause bigger problems later.
- Keep this section focused on time saved and confidence gained.

## 10. Close with the takeaway

- Re-state the practical benefit: Digest Engine helps automate the busywork while keeping humans in charge of the decisions that matter.
- Tie this back to reader trust and editorial quality.
- End with a bridge to related features like relevance training, authority-aware ranking, or composable AI skills.
