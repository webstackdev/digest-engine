# Self-hostable, bring your own models

## Working angle

- Core promise: explain that Digest Engine gives teams control over where the product runs and which AI models it uses.
- Audience lens: marketers and newsletter authors who may not manage infrastructure themselves, but do care about cost, privacy, flexibility, and avoiding vendor lock-in.
- Tone: practical and reassuring. This should feel like freedom of choice, not a deployment manual.
- Suggested headline direction: *Your content, your models, your infra.*

## 1. Open with the fear of lock-in

- Start with a familiar concern: many AI products become harder to trust once your workflow depends on one vendor, one pricing model, or one hosted stack.
- Frame the business risk in plain language: if pricing changes, privacy needs change, or your team wants more control, switching later can be painful.
- Set up Digest Engine’s position clearly: the product is designed so deployment and model choices stay flexible.

## 2. Define the feature in simple terms

- Explain that Digest Engine can be run as a hosted service or self-hosted on your own infrastructure.
- Explain that it can work with different model providers instead of forcing one built-in AI backend.
- Keep the framing user-centered: this means teams can choose the setup that matches their budget, compliance needs, and comfort level.

## 3. Explain why self-hosting matters to non-technical buyers

- Some teams want tighter control over where their content and source data live.
- Some teams have internal policies around external AI services.
- Some teams simply want to reduce long-term operating costs once usage grows.
- Position self-hosting as an option that protects flexibility, even if the team starts with a hosted setup.

## 4. Explain the model-choice story in plain English

- Clarify that Digest Engine is not built around one single model provider.
- Explain that teams can use a hosted gateway like OpenRouter for convenience, or run local models through Ollama when they want more control.
- Keep this non-technical: the important point is that the AI layer is configurable, not hard-wired.
- Emphasize that this makes it easier to adapt as model quality, cost, or policy needs change.

## 5. Show the practical benefit with a simple scenario

- Walk through a realistic progression.
- Example shape: a small team starts with a hosted model setup because it is faster to get going.
- Later, usage grows and they decide to move some or all AI work onto infrastructure they control.
- Show that the value is continuity: the workflow stays familiar even if the deployment setup changes underneath it.

## 6. Connect the feature to data confidence

- Explain that for some teams, especially in marketing or publishing, source lists, internal workflows, and editorial planning data are sensitive assets.
- Make the benefit concrete: if they want to keep that processing inside their own stack, they can.
- Avoid overpromising security claims. Keep it about control, visibility, and optionality.

## 7. Address the cost angle

- Explain that hosted AI is convenient for getting started.
- Explain that self-hosted models can become attractive when teams want more predictable economics.
- Keep the message simple: Digest Engine lets teams optimize for convenience early and control later, instead of forcing a permanent tradeoff.
- Avoid too many precise cost numbers in the outline unless they are used as supporting color.

## 8. Reassure readers who are not technical

- Question to address: "What if I am not the person deploying infrastructure?"
- Answer: that is fine. The value of this feature is still relevant because it means your organization is not boxed into one path.
- Explain that non-technical buyers can still benefit from knowing the product supports both hosted and self-managed models.
- Keep this section focused on buying confidence rather than setup details.

## 9. Position the feature strategically

- Explain that bring-your-own-models is not just a technical nice-to-have. It is a way to future-proof the workflow.
- Tie this to broader product strategy: teams can evolve from experimentation to scale without switching products.
- Reinforce that Digest Engine’s workflow stays stable even when the underlying model choices change.

## 10. Close with the takeaway

- Re-state the practical benefit: Digest Engine lets teams choose the right balance of convenience, cost control, and privacy for their situation.
- Tie the feature back to trust: you can adopt the product without surrendering control over your stack.
- End with a bridge to adjacent features like composable AI skills or pricing/deployment options.

## Notes to preserve from source material

- The key idea to preserve is that the model is a configuration choice, not a hard dependency.
- The contrast between hosted convenience and self-hosted control is central and should remain.
- The final article can mention OpenRouter and Ollama as examples, but the main story should stay focused on user value rather than infrastructure specifics.
- Cost, privacy, and flexibility are the three strongest user-facing themes to carry through the finished article.
